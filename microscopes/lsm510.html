Technical Description

The LSM 510 confocal microscope consits of an inverted Axiovert 200M body for imaging fixed samples and living cells in culture dishes. The microscope has high numerical aperture (NA) lenses (Plan-Neofluar 10x/0.3, Plan-Neofluar 40x/1.3 Oil DIC, C-achroplan NIR 40x/0.8 W and Plan-Apochromat 63x/1.4 Oil DIC), a confocal scanhead (Zeiss LSM510) to select one optical plane at a certain depth in the specimen and make images void of background. As excitation light sources, a set of three lasers: one Argon (458, 477, 488, 514 nm) and two HeNe lasers (543, 633 nm) are in place - which fully cover the whole spectrum of excitation needed for the most commonly used fluorophores. The system is equipped with sensitive photomultiplier tubes (PMT) and a META detector system that enables the researcher to perform spectral un-mixing in case 2 or more fluorescent probes with overlapping spectra are required for the experiment.


Apart from the two microscopes, the facility also has a laminar air flow (LAF) for preparation of cells under sterile conditions, a CO2 incubator for short-term cell culture and a cell-perfusion system that allows users to switch solutions or administer drugs during live-cell-image recordings. The unit is set up to be a multi-user facility, which also offers the advantage that there is expertise around in many applications of fluorescent imaging, including calcium imaging with a variety of sensors, imaging of eGFP (and its mutants), FRAP and multi-color immunocytochemistry.

Terms of use

Training:
Users must be trained before using microscopes independently. To request a training on any of the machines, please contact multiphoton@med.kuleuven.be.

After the training, you will be able to use the booking system with your own university account (u-number), which you will also use to logon to the computer controlling the device.
Charges:
As for all core facilities at the K.U.Leuven, there will be a usage fee. The usage fee will have to cover maintenance costs and the usage of the lasers and depends on the type of equipment.
  • check charges on microscope specific pages, as charges depend heavily on the type of microscope or laser to be used. Ranging from 5 €/hr for widefield systems to 50 €/hr for the most advanced NLO systems.
  • training for high-end machines: 75 € (1.5-2 hours) pp (4 persons minimum per session)
  • extra technical support: 100.00 € per hour (all users)
Urgency fees:
In case experiments need to be performed urgently (implying that changes in booking calendars are requested, training has to be skipped or acutely organized, or ad hoc expert time is required) an urgency surplus charge will apply.
  • surplus charges up to 250€ / event can apply. These charges will not be waived in case of cancellation !
Third party use:
Use of the equipment for third party services (compound screening, drug testing, ....) or whatever other commercial purpose is only allowed after dissusion with the director of the micrsocopy unit (mail), as different hourly charges apply for non-academic imaging.
