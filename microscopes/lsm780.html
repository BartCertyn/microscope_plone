Description of the hardware

1. Zeiss Automated inverted microscope

  • Motorized X,Y stage, Automated filter turret, automated objective turret
  • Minimal Fluorescence unit, LED or Metalhalide lamp
  • Filtercubes: 3 filtercubes (optimized for 1/ GFP/FITC/Alexa488, 2/ RFP/dsRED/mCherry and 3/ for AMCA/DAPI)
  • Top quality high NA, water immersion lenses objective (25 x /63 x) – Suitable both for Vis and IR applications
  • DIC optics

2. Visible light lasers

  • Argon laser (458, 488 & 514 nm), solid state lasers: 405, 561, 633 nm

3. IR laser for NLO Microscopy

  • Mai Tai Deep See Femtosecond Ti:Sa laser, tunable over 680 to 1050 nm
  • Short pulses (< 120fs) over entire wavelength range
  • Predispersion compensation
  • Custom made polarisation control (to be discussed with Pieter Vanden Berghe)

4. Scanning unit:

  • This galvoscanning unit allows capturing at all ViS and IR wavelengths available.

5. Detection

  • 32 highly sensitive GaASP detector array in the descanned beampath, + 2 flanking PMT's for blue and red.
  • Double BIG GaASP detector in a nondescanned beampath both in epi.
  • Double BIG GaASP detector in a nondescanned beampath in forward (use: to be discussed with Pieter Vanden Berghe or Valérie Van Steenbergen)

6. Detection

  • - 0.55 NA air condensor or high NA air condensor (NA 0.8) for recording forward SH signals
  • g. PC and Zen2012 to control
    1. Recording and analysis soft- and hardware, to control scanning, timelapse imaging, triggering, …
    2. PC: RAM 32 Gb, high end multi core processor, W7 64bit, 30 inch screen

7. Communication & triggering

  • I/O TTL communication ports for triggering of external devices like patch clamp amplifiers, stimulators, perfusion equipment should be available…

8. Live cell imaging

  • CO2 incubator + heater that fits or covers the microscope stage. The incubator should be black to avoid influence of ambient light during the experiment.
