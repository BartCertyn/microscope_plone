The fast imaging platform consist of 2 parts:

Description of the hardware

Part 1: spinning disk confocal system (ANDOR revolution)

a. Yokogawa CSU-X1 Spinning Disk Unit, 5000 rpm spinning disk

  • Andor Technology iXon3 DU-897-BV monochrome EMCCD camera. Using EEV CCD97 back-illuminated sensor with peak QE 92%.
  • 512x512 16 micron square pixels, 16bit digitisation, read out speeds 10, 5 and 1 MHz.

b. REVOLUTION 500 series AOTF Laser modulator

  • Laser Module for use with LC 400 to 500 series.
  • Diode laser CW - 405nm, 100 mW, HP410LP dichroic mirror.
  • DPSS CW - 488nm, 50 mW, HP495LP dichroic mirror.
  • DPSS CW - 561nm, 50 mW, HP580LP dichroic mirror.
  • 640 nm, 100mW laser (from January 2014 onwards).

c. Nikon TiE inverted motorized microscope

  • lenses: 20x (NA 0.8, air), 40x (LWD, NA 1.1, WI), 60x (NA1.27, WI)
  • The use of the 40x lens needs to be specified in the booking system !
  • Piezo Z Stage Control for Fast 4D Imaging - 100 um TRAVEL, up to 60 optical sections per sec.

d. Andor Imaging Workstation

  • Andor iQ-CORE-FST 2.9.
  • 4GB RAM and 2x 1 TB (2TB RAID-0) + 500GB 7,200RPM

e. Heating and CO2 controlled incubator

  • Okolabs

Part 2: a CMOS camera (Photron, Focuscope)

a. mounted on an Zeiss Examiner or on Nikon TiE

  • This CMOS camera is capable of recording images at 2 kHz and is therefore ideal to monitor action potential related events, diffusion of fluorescent molecules etc...
